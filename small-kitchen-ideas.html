Small kitchen ideas work best when every piece earns its place.

Start with clear worktops, useful storage and a few good-looking everyday items. The aim is simple: less clutter, more space, and a kitchen that is easy to use.

Use the walls

Open shelves and hanging rails free up drawers and worktops. They are useful for mugs, utensils, jars and small herb pots.

Make cupboards work harder

Under-shelf baskets, pan racks and over-door organisers add storage without adding furniture.

Choose pieces that do more

Glass jars tidy up packaging. A slim trolley fills awkward gaps. A wooden chopping board adds warmth and still gets used every day.

Keep the palette simple

Warm whites, pale wood, glass and soft neutrals help a compact kitchen feel brighter and more ordered.
