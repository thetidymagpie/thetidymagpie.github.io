Beautiful and Useful: The Le Creuset Casserole — and Which One to Buy

There are very few kitchen objects that are genuinely both beautiful and useful. The Le Creuset Signature Cast Iron Casserole is one of the rare ones.

← Back to Journal

I've always been drawn to the idea that everyday objects should be both beautiful and useful — not precious things hidden away for best, but ordinary things that quietly do their job while still bringing some kind of pleasure to the room. The Le Creuset casserole dish earns its place on the hob, on the table and on the open shelf. It doesn't need to be hidden away. That matters more than people give it credit for.

It's the kind of object the Mingei philosophy was made for — honest, simple, made to be used. A bowl, a jug, a cast iron pot. Things that age well and make everyday routines feel a little more considered.

Why it's worth the money

Let's be honest about the price first, because it is significant. A Le Creuset Signature Cast Iron Round Casserole starts at around £150 and goes up depending on size. That's not an impulse purchase.

But here's the thing — it's made in France, comes with a lifetime guarantee, and if you look after it properly, you will genuinely never need to buy another one. When you divide the cost over twenty or thirty years of daily use, it becomes one of the better value things in the kitchen.

It holds heat extraordinarily well. Cast iron absorbs heat slowly and evenly, which means it's forgiving in a way that thinner pans simply aren't. It works on every hob including induction, goes straight from hob to oven, and the tight-fitting lid locks in moisture so that slow-cooked things come out tasting like you spent far more effort than you did. The enamel interior means there's no seasoning required — you just use it, wash it, and put it away. Or leave it out. It looks that good.

What you can cook in it

Almost everything, which sounds like an exaggeration but isn't. In a Le Creuset casserole you can make:

  • Slow-cooked casseroles and stews
  • Soups and broths
  • One-pot pasta and risotto
  • Curries and braised meat
  • No-knead bread — genuinely excellent, with a proper crust
  • Baked fruit and puddings

The size you choose determines what's practical, which is where most people get stuck.

If you're buying one as your only casserole dish, the 26cm is the safe choice for most households. Versatile enough for family cooking without being unwieldy.

Which colour?

Le Creuset colours are one of the quiet joys of owning one. A few thoughts on the current range:

  • Volcanic — the original, iconic orange. Warm and timeless, goes with almost everything.
  • Cerise — a rich cherry red. Makes a kitchen feel alive.
  • Deep Teal / Azure — cooler tones that sit beautifully in more pared-back kitchens.
  • Sage / Flint — softer, quieter. If your kitchen is neutral and calm, these blend in without demanding attention.

There's no wrong answer. The one you'll use is the right one.

Is it a good gift?

One of the best kitchen gifts you can give someone, particularly for a significant occasion — a wedding, a housewarming, a big birthday. It's the kind of gift that gets used every week and lasts longer than almost anything else you could buy. The 26cm in a colour that suits their kitchen is usually a safe and genuinely appreciated choice.
