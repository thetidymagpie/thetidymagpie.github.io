The Rustic Outdoor Kitchen

Stone walls, wooden shelves, terracotta pots and the smell of herbs in the sun — outdoor cooking at its most beautiful.

An outdoor kitchen works best when it feels like it has always been there — nothing too polished, nothing too designed. The best ones have a heaviness to them: a solid table, pots that have weathered a few seasons, wooden bowls worn soft with use. Materials that improve with age rather than fight against it.

The details that make the difference are usually small. Herbs growing close enough to snip straight into a pan. A decent olive oil pourer within reach. Plates that feel good in your hand. Stack a few blue ceramic plates on a wooden shelf and suddenly the whole space feels considered without trying too hard.
