The bathroom is probably the most overlooked room in the house.

It's the first place you go in the morning and the last place you're in at night. You use it every single day. And yet it's usually the room people decorate last, spend the least on, and think about the least.

A calm, lovely bathroom makes a genuine difference to how a day starts and ends. It doesn't take much either — mostly just a bit of editing and a few things chosen carefully rather than grabbed in a hurry.

The details worth focusing on

  • white or cream towels, properly fluffy and folded neatly
  • a woven basket for storage instead of plastic bins
  • one beautiful thing on the windowsill — an orchid, a candle, a small plant
  • glass jars for cotton pads and bath salts instead of original packaging
  • a wooden bath stool or bath tray if there’s space
  • soft neutral colours that don’t compete with each other
  • gentle lighting, or at least a candle for evenings

The thing that makes the biggest difference is reducing visual noise. Decanting products into simple containers, hiding clutter under the sink, keeping surfaces mostly clear apart from a few lovely things you genuinely use every day.

A bathroom that feels spa-like usually isn't expensively fitted.

It's just quieter than most.
