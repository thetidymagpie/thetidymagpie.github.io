The anchor piece for the coffee corner. A proper worktop machine makes the space feel intentional rather than like a collection of loose mugs and jars.

Best for storage

Useful for coffee beans, sugar, biscuits or pods. Glass jars make a coffee shelf feel calmer and more organised.

Best everyday detail

Matching mugs help the corner feel considered without overstyling it. Good for open shelving or a small worktop setup.

Best finishing touch

A tray gathers the machine, jars and mugs into one clear zone so the coffee corner looks tidy rather than cluttered.

Best non-machine option

A simple classic for slower mornings, guests or anyone who wants good coffee without another appliance.

Back to Journal

A coffee corner gives a kitchen one small, useful ritual zone.

Keep the machine, mugs and everyday extras together so mornings feel easier and the worktop looks more intentional.

Choose one spot

A short run of worktop, a shelf, a tray or a slim sideboard can all work. The area only needs to hold what is used most.

Keep it simple

Use a mug rack, small jars, a tray and one good container for pods, beans or spoons.

Add warmth

Wood, ceramic, glass and linen make a coffee station feel softer than a row of appliances alone.

Kitchen pieces worth keeping

The most useful kitchen corners are built from pieces that work every day and still look good left out.
